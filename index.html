Missile Woman of India:Tessy Thomas



Known as: “Missile Woman of India” Project Director for Agni-IV missile at DRDO. First woman to lead an Indian missile project. A role model in defense engineering and space research.

  • Worked on the Agni series of ballistic missiles.
  • Became the Project Director of Agni-IV—a long-range missile capable of carrying nuclear warheads.
  • First Indian woman to lead such a high-level missile project.
  • Later, led Agni-V, India’s intercontinental ballistic missile.

Other Inspiring Engineers

Edith Clarke (USA)

Known as: First female electrical engineer in the U.S. Invented the Clarke calculator used in power transmission. First woman to teach engineering at a U.S. university.

Sudha Murty (India)

Known as: First female engineer at TELCO Broke gender norms in the 1970s. Later became a philanthropist and chairperson of the Infosys Foundation. Inspires through her engineering background and social work..

G. Anuradha

Project Director at ISRO’s Space Applications Centre. Led teams working on satellite payload development.

A. Lalitha

India’s first female electrical engineer (graduated in 1943). Worked on power generation and transmission; a pioneer for Indian women in technical fields.

Emily Warren Roebling (USA)

Managed the construction of the Brooklyn Bridge after her husband (chief engineer) became ill. Learned civil engineering informally and played a vital on-site role.

Gitanjali Rao (USA-India origin)

Young inventor and engineer; named TIME Kid of the Year in 2020. Developed innovations like a lead detection device for water and anti-cyberbullying tech.
